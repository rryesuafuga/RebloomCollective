The Challenge

Three Interconnected Crises

In Eastern Uganda's Bukedi sub-region, gender-based violence, period poverty, and environmental degradation form a deadly cycle that traps women and girls in poverty.

Gender-Based Violence

37.1% Sexual violence rate in Bukedi
  • 58% of survivors tell no one (highest silence rate in Uganda)
  • Economic dependence keeps women trapped in abusive relationships
  • Limited trauma-informed services in local languages

Period Poverty

65% Cannot afford menstrual products
  • 85% resort to unhygienic cloth or rags
  • 28% of girls miss school during menstruation
  • 23% drop out after menstruation begins

Environmental Degradation

500+ Years for one pad to decompose
  • Zero reusable pad manufacturers in the region
  • Plastic waste accumulates in communities
  • No local sustainable alternatives available
"

I like him... he's the source of income. If I let him go to prison, I am not going to get any money.

- GBV Survivor, Pallisa District Illustrating why economic dependence keeps women trapped in abusive relationships

Our Approach

The Three-Pillar Model

An integrated approach that addresses all three crises simultaneously, creating a self-sustaining cycle of healing, empowerment, and environmental protection.

01

GBV Rehabilitation Center

Trauma-informed counseling and psychosocial support delivered in local languages (Ateso, Lugwere) by trained professionals.

  • Individual & group counseling sessions
  • Crisis intervention & safety planning
  • Community reintegration support
  • Family mediation services
02

Rebloom Pads Production

Survivor-led manufacturing of high-quality reusable menstrual pads, providing both income and purpose through meaningful work.

  • 8-week comprehensive training program
  • Fair wages for all production work
  • Quality control & business skills
  • Pathway to entrepreneurship
03

CHE Network

Community Health Entrepreneurs provide last-mile distribution while delivering menstrual health education and GBV awareness.

  • 20-25% commission on sales
  • MHM education delivery
  • GBV awareness outreach
  • Pathway for graduated survivors
The Pathway

From Crisis to Empowerment

A structured 8-month journey that transforms survivors from crisis into confident entrepreneurs and community leaders.

1 Week 1

Referral & Intake

Assessment, safety planning, and immediate support needs identification.

2 Weeks 2-8

Healing Phase

Individual counseling, group therapy, and psychosocial support in local languages.

3 Weeks 9-16

Skills Training

Comprehensive pad production training covering pattern cutting, sewing, and quality control.

4 Month 5+

Employment

Paid production work with fair wages, or receive a starter kit for independent business.

5 Month 8+

CHE Opportunity

Become a Community Health Entrepreneur, running your own distribution business.

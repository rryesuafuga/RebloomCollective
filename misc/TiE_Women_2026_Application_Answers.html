Business Description

Q Tell us the broad industry sector in which you compete or will compete *

Healthcare / Social Impact / Sustainable Consumer Products

Rebloom Collective operates at the intersection of three sectors: (1) Healthcare — menstrual health and hygiene management (MHM); (2) Social Impact — trauma-informed support and economic empowerment for gender-based violence (GBV) survivors; and (3) Sustainable Consumer Products — eco-friendly reusable menstrual pad manufacturing.

Q Please describe your product or service. What is your Unique Selling Proposition? *

Rebloom Collective is Eastern Uganda's first integrated social enterprise simultaneously tackling gender-based violence, period poverty, and environmental degradation through a unique Three-Pillar Model:

Pillar 1 — GBV Rehabilitation Center

Trauma-informed counseling and psychosocial support delivered in local languages (Ateso, Lugwere) by trained professionals, offering individual therapy, group peer support, crisis intervention, and community reintegration.

Pillar 2 — Rebloom Pads Production

High-quality reusable menstrual pad kits (4 pads + waterproof pouch) manufactured by trained GBV survivors. Each kit costs ~$4 USD (UGX 15,000), lasts 2–3 years, and replaces 200+ disposable pads — 70% cheaper over its lifetime.

Pillar 3 — CHE (Community Health Entrepreneur) Network

Last-mile distribution through trained women entrepreneurs who earn 20–25% commission and simultaneously deliver menstrual health education and GBV awareness to remote communities.

Unique Selling Proposition

We are the only organisation that transforms GBV survivors from victims into entrepreneurs while solving period poverty and reducing environmental waste. Our survivor-led production model creates a powerful empowerment cycle: women heal → gain skills → earn income → become independent business owners. No competitor in the region combines all three outcomes in a single, self-sustaining enterprise.

Q Please briefly describe your customer acquisition strategy? *

Our go-to-market strategy combines four synergistic channels:

1. CHE Network (primary channel — 18% of Y1 revenue, growing to dominant channel)

Train and deploy 25 Community Health Entrepreneurs in Year 1, scaling to 100 by Year 3. CHEs earn 20–25% commission, creating strong financial incentive to sell. Door-to-door reach penetrates villages inaccessible to traditional retail. CHEs also deliver free MHM education, building trust before pitching products.

2. Institutional / Bulk Sales (32% of Y1 revenue)

Partner with NGOs implementing MHM programmes (UNFPA, WaterAid, local CBOs), district schools for student health initiatives, and health centres for patient distribution. Offer 25% bulk discount with co-branded education materials.

3. Direct Retail (35% of Y1 revenue)

Sales from the production centre, market-day presence in trading centres, and referral-driven word-of-mouth from satisfied customers.

4. Training & Workshops (15% of Y1 revenue)

Fee-based MHM education sessions for schools and communities; pad-making skills workshops; capacity-building for partner organisations.

Retention Strategy

The 2–3 year product lifespan creates a natural repurchase cycle. Ongoing CHE relationships maintain direct customer contact, and quality products build lasting brand loyalty.
